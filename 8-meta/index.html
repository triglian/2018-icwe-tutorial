When to ask questions?

When to ask questions?

  • Right after the explanation (short-term recall, early feedback, adapt/re-explain)
  • Before the explanation (test previous knowledge, motivate the students to listen to the explanation)
  • At the beginning of the lecture (warm up, long-term recall, survey the audience)
  • Before the break (reduce boredom for fast students, more time to answer and process the answers, discuss solutions after the break is over)
  • End of the lecture (medium-term recall, summarize, introduce homework, cliffhanger for next lecture)

What type of questions to ask?

Short-answer Questions

  1. Yes/No? Right/Wrong? (Verification)
  2. A/B? (Disjunction)
  3. How Many? (Quantification)
  4. Who? What? (Concept completion)

Long-answer Questions

  1. What does it mean? (Definition)
  2. How does this compares to that? (Comparison)
  3. Is it good or bad? (Judgment)
  4. What was the cause? (Antecedent)
  5. What if? (Consequence)
  6. What for? (Goal orientation)
  7. How does it work? (Instrumental/Procedural)
  8. Which is similar to which? (Classification)
  9. What makes it possible? (Enablement)
  10. Why? (Explanation)
  11. Why not? (Expectational)

Open-ended Questions

  1. Can you give an example of it? (Exemplification)
  2. Which ways? (Enumeration)
  3. What happened? (Interpretation)
  4. What is more/less important? (Prioritization)
  5. Which decision would you take? (Design)
  6. Can it be done? (Feasibility)
  7. How to do it? (Problem Solving)
  8. What would you say? (Brainstorming)
  9. What is wrong with this? (Critique)
Why ask questions?

Why ask Questions?

Students

  • Engage/interact with the taught material to find an answer (active learning)

Professors

  • Gain awareness about the understanding of the whole class
  • Give feedback to the whole class
  • Discover misconceptions
  • Diagnose and repair knowledge deficits
  • Give students something to do while you take a short break
Why answer questions?

Why answer Questions?

Students

  • Get immediate feedback
  • Take up the challenge
  • Share knowledge
  • Contribute to the discussion
  • Compare solutions
  • Leave your mark on the beamer
  • Get a reward (external motivation)
